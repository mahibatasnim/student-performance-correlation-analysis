## Key Findings
1. Failures exhibit a moderate negative correlation with final grade (r ≈ -0.36).
2. All the study time shows a weak positive correlation with final grade (r ≈ 0.09).
3. Absences shows almost no correlation with final grade (r ≈ 0.03).
 
## Conclusion
The project analysis clearly states that among the variables,academic failures have the most substantial negative impact on 
students final grades. Which suggests that previously done academic difficulties strongly predict lower performance. Study time 
appears to have minor positive influence which points that efforts are useful to get better grades. Shockingly, absences exhibits almost 
no correlation with final grades. So, only attendance is not a reliable predictor of performance. All these findings provide clear insight 
into which factors affect students the most and which almost does not affect at all in student achievement and not to mention it also can 
guide further research interventios.
